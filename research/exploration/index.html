    Research Exploration: The 3D Vision Frontier

    ——reconstructing the world from pixels to voxels 🌌

    This page documents my journey into Computer Vision and Embodied AI. My goal is to move beyond 2D image processing and explore how machines can perceive, represent, and interact with the 3D physical world.

    📝 To-Do List (Freshman Year & Beyond)

    • Foundations: Mastering Image Formation, Epipolar Geometry, and Camera Calibration.
    • Deep Learning: Understanding CNNs, Transformers, and MLP-based architectures.
    • Neural Rendering: Diving deep into 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) and NeRF.
    • Lab Integration: Preparing for research in 3D reconstruction and spatial intelligence.

    💡 Core Research Interests

    1. Neural Graphics & 3DGS

    Traditional meshes are rigid. I am fascinated by point-based and volume-based representations that allow for real-time, high-fidelity rendering. 3D Gaussian Splatting represents a breakthrough in balancing speed and quality.

    2. Spatial Intelligence

    How does an agent understand "where" it is? I am exploring the intersection of SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping) and deep features to build more robust environmental priors for robots.

    3. Embodied AI

    The ultimate test of vision is action. I am interested in how 3D representations can be used for robot manipulation and navigation in unstructured, real-world environments.

    🏗️ The Learning Path

    Phase 1: The Geometry (Current)

    • Concepts: Projective Geometry, Essential/Fundamental Matrices, Bundle Adjustment.
    • Tools: OpenCV, Eigen, C++.

    Phase 2: The Neural Era

    • Concepts: Neural Radiance Fields (NeRF), Differentiable Rendering, Point-based Neural Rendering.
    • Tools: PyTorch, CUDA, Tiny-CUDA-NN.

    Phase 3: The Interaction

    • Concepts: Reinforcement Learning for Vision, Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models.
    • Tools: Isaac Gym, Habitat-Sim.

    🌐 Paper Radar & Resources

    📄 Milestone Papers
