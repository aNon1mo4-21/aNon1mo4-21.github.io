    Mathematical Analysis: The Calculus of Rigor

    ——where intuition meets \(\epsilon - \delta\) 🔍

    Mathematical Analysis is the formalization of "closeness." For a CS student interested in 3D Vision and Embodied AI, this isn't just about solving integrals—it's about understanding the continuity and optimization of the physical world.

    📝 To-Do List (Spring 2026)

    • Multivariable Calculus: Partial derivatives, Gradients, and the Jacobian matrix.
    • Sequences & Series: Understanding convergence and Taylor approximations.
    • Optimization Foundations: Lagrange multipliers and Hessian matrices for surface analysis.
    • Measure Theory: Long-term goal for a deeper understanding of probability.

    💡 Key Intuitions

    The Linear Approximation

    A derivative isn't just a slope; it's the best linear approximation of a function at a point. In 3DGS or Neural Rendering, we constantly linearize complex systems to make them computable.

    From 1D to \(\mathbb{R}^n\)

    The jump from single-variable to multivariable analysis is where the magic happens.

    • The Gradient (\(\nabla f\)): Points in the direction of steepest ascent.
    • The Divergence (\(\nabla \cdot F\)): Measuring the "source" or "sink" of a vector field.
    • The Curl (\(\nabla \times F\)): Essential for understanding rotations in 3D space.

    🛠️ Essential Formulae

    \[L(x) = f(a) + f'(a)(x - a)\]
    \[\nabla f(a) = \left( \frac{\partial f}{\partial x_1}(a), \dots, \frac{\partial f}{\partial x_n}(a) \right)\]
