## 💡 Key Intuitions

## The Linear Approximation

A derivative isn't just a slope; it's the **best linear approximation** of a function at a point. In 3DGS or Neural Rendering, we constantly linearize complex systems to make them computable.

## From 1D to $\mathbb{R}^n$

The jump from single-variable to multivariable analysis is where the magic happens.

- **The Gradient ($\nabla f$)**: Points in the direction of steepest ascent.
- **The Divergence ($\nabla \cdot F$)**: Measuring the "source" or "sink" of a vector field.
- **The Curl ($\nabla \times F$)**: Essential for understanding rotations in 3D space.
